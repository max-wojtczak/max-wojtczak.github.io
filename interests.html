Current Interests

  • Programming

    • Having just started serious programming dring my freshman year of college, I'm most fluent in Java. However, I've also dabbled with, and have beginner level understandings of Python, Javascript, PHP, and Go.

  • Computer Hardware

    • I decided in my freshman year of high school that the challenge of building my own custom computer would probably be something that I'd very much enjoy. Even though it took me almost three years to complete the build, I loved every second of it. From that point on I've been intrigued by the not only the basic functions of CPUs, GPUs, RAM, and so forth, but also by concepts in computer engineering like Moore's Law, hashing, and bit flipping.

  • News/Information

    • Far from being what's known as a news junkie, I suppose a more apt term for me would be info glutton. I don't like to stop at just the news for my daily dose of current events. Anything from tech sites to forum discussions to economics blogs all fuel my thirst for information. Among the major publications that I read are the New York Times, the Wall Street Journal, the Washington Post, Forbes, Business Insider, the Associated Press, and Arstechnica.

  • Gaming

    • It's my firm belief that every computer scientist should be a gamer in some way. Whether it's all the latest and greatest consoles and newly released games, or timeless classics for the Commodore 64 or original Atari, everyone has a gamer inside of them. Personally, I opt for the best of both worlds, playing games like Mario 64 and Super Smash Bros on the NES, and using a current generation Xbox One and PlayStation 4 for newer titles like Dead Rising 4, The Division, and Death Stranding.
